ABOUT THE LEGEND

Allah Rakha Rahman was born as A. S. Dileep Kumar in Madras, Tamil Nadu, on 6 January 1967. His father, R. K. Shekhar, from a Mudaliar family, was a film-score composer and conductor for Tamil and Malayalam films.
Rahman began studying piano at age four. He assisted his father in the studio, playing the piano and learning about music production.

After his father's death when Rahman was nine years old, the rental of his father's musical equipment provided his family's income. Raised by his mother, Kareema (born Kashturi), Rahman, who was studying at Padma Seshadri Bala Bhavan, had to work to support his family, which led him to routinely miss classes and fail exams.

In an interview in 2012, Rahman said that his mother was summoned and was told to take him to the streets of Kodambakkam to beg and not to send him to school.

Rahman attended another school called MCN for a year, and later joined the Madras Christian College Higher Secondary School, where he was admitted based on his musical talent. He formed a band with his high school classmates. However, after discussing with his mother, he later dropped out of school to pursue a career as a full-time musician.

Rahman was a keyboard player and arranger for bands such as Roots (with childhood friend and percussionist Sivamani, John Anthony, Suresh Peters, JoJo, and Raja) and founded the Chennai-based rock group Nemesis Avenue. He mastered the keyboard, piano, synthesizer, harmonium, and guitar, and was particularly interested in the synthesizer because it was the "ideal combination of music and technology."

AWARDS AND ACHIEVEMENTS

  • Academy Awards: Rahman won two Oscars for Best Original Score and Best Original Song for the movie Slumdog Millionaire in 2009.
  • Grammy Awards: He received two Grammy Awards for Slumdog Millionaire in 2010.
  • BAFTA Award: Best Film Music for Slumdog Millionaire in 2009.
  • Golden Globe Award: Best Original Score for Slumdog Millionaire in 2009.
  • National Film Awards: Rahman has won six National Film Awards, which include Best Music Direction for films like Roja (1992), Minsara Kanavu (1997), and Lagaan (2001).
  • Filmfare Awards: Rahman has won numerous Filmfare Awards in both Bollywood and South Indian cinema, including the Best Music Director award for Roja, Bombay, and Lagaan.
  • Padma Bhushan: The Government of India honored Rahman with the Padma Bhushan in 2010, one of the country's highest civilian awards.
  • Padma Shri: He was awarded the Padma Shri in 2000 for his contributions to Indian music.

LEGACY

A.R. Rahman's influence extends beyond music; he is also a cultural ambassador for India. His music has bridged the gap between Indian and international music, making him one of the most celebrated and respected composers worldwide.

Rahman is known for his philanthropic efforts, including his involvement in various social causes and his support for education and music through the A.R. Rahman Foundation and the KM Music Conservatory in Chennai.

His contribution to music and his ability to blend traditional Indian music with electronic sounds, orchestral arrangements, and world music have earned him the nickname "Mozart of Madras."
